MONKEYPOX

What is Monkeypox?

Monkeypox is a double stranded DNA virus which causes Humans and Animals to get Monkeypox.Monkeypox belongs to the genus Orthopoxvirus in the family Poxvirdae.Monkeypox is one of the human orthopoxvirus along with 'Variola', 'Cowpox' and 'Vaccinia' viruses. Monkeypox is very similar to a old virus 'Smallpox' but with a milder rash and a lower morality rate.

Where and When did Monkeypox originate?

The first human case of monkeypox was in 1970 in the Democratic Republic of the Congo when a 9-month old child was found with Monkeypox. However, the first case of Monkeypox in general was in 1958 when it was found in monkeys being kept in a lab

What are the symptoms for MonkeyPox?

The symptoms for Monkeypox are
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Exhaustion
  • Muscle aches and backache
  • Respiratory symptoms
    • How does Monkey Pox Spread?

    Monkeypox spreads through contact between an infected person and another person through rashes, wounds, scabs, and bodily fluids. Or by close intimate contact
